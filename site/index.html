How MarketPulse works

Two parts, both grounded in the same daily-updated data:

  1. Index Monitor вҖ” latest level, today's move, and year-to-date change for fifteen world indices, plus a price-history chart. "Compare all (rebased to 100)" normalizes every index to a common starting point so relative performance is directly comparable.
  2. Monte Carlo Simulator вҖ” projects an index forward using Geometric Brownian Motion:
S(t+1) = S(t) В· exp( (Ој вҲ’ ВҪПғВІ)В·dt + ПғВ·вҲҡdtВ·Z ),   Z ~ N(0,1),  dt = 1/252

Ој (annual drift) and Пғ (annual volatility) are calibrated from each index's trailing one year of daily log-returns вҖ” recomputed every day. The simulator draws hundreds to thousands of independent paths, then reports the median outcome, the 5th-percentile level (a Value-at-Risk style downside), the 95th percentile, and P(loss) вҖ” the share of paths ending below today's level.

GBM assumes constant drift/volatility and normally-distributed returns. Real markets have fat tails, regime shifts, and jumps, so treat the bands as a calibrated illustration of dispersion вҖ” not a prediction.
