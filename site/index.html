How MarketPulse works

Two parts, both grounded in the same daily-updated data:

  1. Index Monitor — latest level, today's move, and year-to-date change for fifteen world indices, plus a price-history chart. "Compare all (rebased to 100)" normalizes every index to a common starting point so relative performance is directly comparable.
  2. Monte Carlo Simulator — projects an index forward using Geometric Brownian Motion:
S(t+1) = S(t) · exp( μ·dt + σ·√dt·Z ),   Z ~ N(0,1),  dt = 1/252

μ (the annualized mean of daily log-returns) and σ (annualized volatility) are calibrated from each index's trailing one year of daily log-returns — recomputed every day. Because μ is measured directly from log-returns, it already reflects the usual GBM variance drag (μ = true annual growth rate − ½σ²), so no further adjustment is applied per step. The simulator draws hundreds to thousands of independent paths, then reports the median outcome, the 5th-percentile level (a Value-at-Risk style downside), the 95th percentile, and P(loss) — the share of paths ending below today's level.

GBM assumes constant drift/volatility and normally-distributed returns. Real markets have fat tails, regime shifts, and jumps, so treat the bands as a calibrated illustration of dispersion — not a prediction.

⚠ Educational / research instrument. Index data refreshes daily, not in real time; the source is an unofficial free endpoint. Simulations are illustrative. Not financial advice.

A poor-man's efficient frontier: each faint dot is a random split across your invested indices; your portfolio is the highlighted one.

⚠ Read before trusting these numbers: all returns are local-currency price returns — no FX conversion, no dividends, no fees or taxes. A portfolio blending indices priced in different currencies (e.g. S&P 500 in USD, Nikkei 225 in JPY) measures a blend of index performance, not a return you could actually earn by holding these instruments. Indices are aligned on the intersection of trading dates across whichever ones you've weighted — a date any one of them didn't trade drops out of the whole backtest, rather than being forward-filled. Indices with less history than your chosen start date are excluded from the run and flagged above, never silently folded into the others. Educational tool, not investment advice.

⚠ Read before trusting these numbers: all returns are local-currency price returns — no FX conversion, no dividends, no fees or taxes, same as the Backtester tab. The correlation matrix and volatility lines use each index's own trading calendar; the portfolio VaR/CVaR aligns on the intersection of trading dates across whichever indices carry nonzero weight. Historical VaR/CVaR assume the future will resemble the historical window used — they describe how this exact portfolio mix would have fared on days already in the data, not a guarantee about tomorrow; a regime change, crisis, or newly-listed index with a short history can make the real tail risk larger than history suggests. Uninvested weight (if any) is held as 0-return cash, never silently redistributed. Educational tool, not investment advice.
